Nasi Lemak

Description

Nasi lemak is a fragrant coconut rice dish traditionally served with spicy sambal, crispy anchovies, roasted peanuts, cucumber, and boiled egg. This version is simple and beginner friendly.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups jasmine rice, rinsed
  • 1 1/2 cups coconut milk
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 pandan leaves, knotted (optional)
  • 1 slice ginger
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup sambal (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1/2 cup fried anchovies (ikan bilis)
  • 1/2 cup roasted peanuts
  • 1 cucumber, sliced
  • 4 boiled eggs, halved

Steps

  1. Add rice, coconut milk, water, pandan leaves, ginger, and salt to a rice cooker.
  2. Cook until done, then let it rest for 10 minutes before fluffing with a fork.
  3. Warm sambal in a small pan over low heat for 2 to 3 minutes if serving hot.
  4. Prepare side items: fry anchovies if needed, roast peanuts, boil eggs, and slice cucumber.
  5. To serve, plate coconut rice and arrange sambal, anchovies, peanuts, cucumber, and egg around it.
  6. Serve immediately while the rice is warm.
